Land Birds of Melbourne

Rock dove

Columba livia (Columbidae)

29 to 37 cm. In human habitation areas. Introduced species.

Crested pigeon

Ocyphaps lophotes (Columbidae)

30 to 34 cm. In lightly wooded grasslands. When flying from danger the wings make a whistling sound.

Spotted dove

Spilopelia chinensis (Columbidae)

12 to 19 cm. In parks and gardens. Introduced species.

Sulphur-crested cockatoo

Cacatua galerita (Cacatuidae)

44 to 55 cm. In wooded areas.

Galah

Eolophus roseicapilla (Cacatuidae)

35 cm. In wooded areas except rainforests.

Musk lorikeet

Glossopsitta concinna (Psittaculidae)

22 cm. In wooded areas.

Eastern rosella

Platycercus eximius (Psittaculidae)

28 to 32 cm. In open woodlands, grasslands.

Rainbow Lorikeet

Trichoglossus moluccanus (Psittaculidae)

25 to 30 cm. In wooded areas.

Superb fairywren

Malurus cyaneus (Maluridae)

14 cm. In dense shrubby areas. The head and back is blue and black in breeding males, and brown in females and non-breeding males.

Spotted pardalote

Pardalotus punctatus (Pardalotidae)

8 to 10 cm. In the canopy of wooded areas.

Brown thornbill

Acanthiza pusilla (Acanthizidae)

9 to 10 cm. In dense shrubby areas.

White-browed scrubwren

Sericornis frontalis (Acanthizidae)

11 to 14 cm. In dense shrubby areas.

Red wattlebird

Anthochaera carunculata (Meliphagidae)

33 to 37 cm. In wooded areas and gardens.

Little wattlebird

Anthochaera chrysoptera (Meliphagidae)

26 to 30 cm. In shrubby areas.

Noisey Miner

Manorina melanocephala (Meliphagidae)

24 to 28 cm. In wooded areas with sparse shrubs.

White-plumed honeyeater

Ptilotula penicillata (Meliphagidae)

15 to 17 cm. In wooded areas often near water.

Welcome swallow

Hirundo neoxena (Hirundinidae)

15 cm. Anywhere except dense forests.

Common blackbird

Turdus merula (Turdidae)

24 to 29 cm. In human habitation areas. Introduced species.

Common myna

Acridotheres tristis (Sturnidae)

23 to 26 cm. In human habitation areas. Introduced species.

Common starling

Sturnus vulgaris (Sturnidae)

19 to 23 cm. In human habitation areas. Introduced species.

Grey butcherbird

Cracticus torquatus (Artamidae)

27 to 30 cm. In wooded areas.

Australian magpie

Gymnorhina tibicen (Artamidae)

37 to 43 cm. In grasslands with nearby trees.

Pied Currawong

Strepera graculina (Artamidae)

44 to 50 cm. In wooded and suburban areas.

Little Raven

Corvus mellori (Corvidae)

48 to 50 cm. In grassland, open areas.

Magpie-lark

Grallina cyanoleuca (Monarchidae)

25 to 30 cm. Anywhere except rainforests.

Grey fantail

Rhipidura albiscapa (Rhipiduridae)

16 cm. In wooded areas.

Willie wagtail

Rhipidura leucophrys (Rhipiduridae)

20 cm. In wooded areas near water.
